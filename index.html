This portfolio project invoves in-depth analysis of dataset given to exteact insights for a fitness-based company - Bellabeat. The analysis follows the six steps of Data Analytics : Ask, Prepare, Process, Analyse, Share and Act.

Background information

Bellabeat is a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart products. Their target market is women's health. Urška Sršen and Sando Mur are the cofounders of Bellabeat. Sršen used her background as an artist to develop beautifully designed technology that informs and inspires women around the world. Collecting data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health has allowed Bellabeat to empower women with knowledge about their own health and habits. Since it was founded in 2013, Bellabeat has grown rapidly and quickly positioned itself as a tech-driven wellness company for women. Sršen is confident that an analysis of non-Bellebeat consumer data (ie. FitBit fitness tracker usage data) would reveal more opportunities for the company's growth.

A: Business Task

The business task is to analyse the fit-bit database of 30 volunteering candidates and identifying trends in the smart device usage, then reciprocating the results in order to unlock new growth opportunities and to improve the marketing strategy of Bellabeat.

Key Stakeholders

  • Urska Srsen - Cofounder of Bellabeat
  • Sando Mur - Cofounder of Bellabeat
  • Marketing Analytics Team
  • Existing and Potential customers of Bellabeat

B: Data Preparation

Data Source

  • Data is stored in the Kaggle Public Dataset in 18 different files in the Excel and CSV formats.
  • The dataset involves health and fitness based fit-bit data of 30 volunteering candidates.
  • The dataset is generated by respondents to a distributed survey via Amazon Mechanical Turk between 03.12.2016-05.12.2016
  • Data collected includes minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring.

Limitations of the Dataset

  • The dataset involves readings of only 30 individuals, which is not representative of the entire fitness community.
  • The dataset is quite old (2016), therefore might not be representative of the current trends.

D: Data Analysis

The next step in the process is to manipulate the data to make it easier to analyse.

  • Joins

    This process involves combining multiple relations (or tables) in the dataset to further support in depth analysis.

    For Example, - (in R)

    In order to analyse sleep data with daily activity, the 'sleep_day_merged' and 'daily_activity_merged'datasets are inner joined into a single dataset 'sleep_step_rel'.

    First, the 'tidyverse' library is installed and called.

                            
    install.packages("tidyverse")
    library(tidyverse)

    Then the datasets are imported to R Studio and assigned to variables.

                            
    daily_activity <- read_xlsx("Bellabeat/daily_activity_merged.xlsx")
    sleep_data <- read_xlsx('Bellabeat/sleep_day_merged.xlsx')

    After this, both the datasets undergo some data manipulation (covered in next section), and are inner joined on the basis of Id and ActivityDate. 

                            
    #Total Minutes Manipulation
    daily_activity <- daily_activity %>% 
        mutate(totalMinutes = VeryActiveMinutes+FairlyActiveMinutes+LightlyActiveMinutes+SedentaryMinutes)

    #Sleep Date Manipulation
    sleep_data$SleepDay <- as.Date(sleep_data$SleepDay)
    sleep_data <- sleep_data %>%
        mutate (SleepRatio = TotalMinutesAsleep / TotalTimeInBed)

    #Inner Join
    sleep_step_rel <- inner_join(daily_activity, sleep_data, by = c('ActivityDate', 'Id'))

    Therefore, the new dataset contins the data of both the daily activity and sleep for any given Id and Date.

    • Or in Sql

                        
    SELECT *
    FROM `bellabeat-393115.daily_activity.daily_acitvity_updated_csv` as daily_activity
    INNER JOIN `bellabeat-393115.daily_activity.sleep_data` as sleep_data 
    ON daily_activity.Id = sleep_data.Id AND daily_activity.ActivityDate = sleep_data.ActivityDate

    Join can be done using SQL as well. In the above code, both the tables (daily_activity and sleep_data) are selected and inner joined on the basis of Id and ActivityDate.

  • Data Manipulation

    This proces involves converting and manipulating the data within the dataset to make the analysis easier.

    For Example - In the previous step of inner join, some data manipulation is done in the daily_activity and sleep_data variables to allow easier analysis.

                            
    #Total Minutes Manipulation
    daily_activity <- daily_activity %>% 
    mutate(totalMinutes = VeryActiveMinutes+FairlyActiveMinutes+LightlyActiveMinutes+SedentaryMinutes)

    The LightlyActiveMinutes column gives the total amount of minutes spent slightly active throughout the day. Similarly, the VeryActiveMinutes, FairlyActiveMinutes and SedentaryMinutes all calculate their respective values. However, we are interested in total minutes logged in to the tracker, hence a new column called 'TotalMinutes' is added to the dataset which adds all the time values.

                            
    #Sleep Date Manipulation
    sleep_data$SleepDay <- as.Date(sleep_data$SleepDay)
    sleep_data <- sleep_data %>%
    mutate (SleepRatio = TotalMinutesAsleep / TotalTimeInBed)

    This manipultion involves changing the dataset of the SleepDay column, initially from String type to Date type. A new column 'SleepRatio' is created which is the ratio between Totl time spent sleeping vy total time in bed. it is basically a measure of quality of sleep. Higer the sleep ratio, better the sleep quality.

  • Statistical Calculations

    Statistical measures like mean, median, standard deviation, etc. are calculated to get some insights.

    For example, in the inner joined dataset which consists of data involving daily steps, intensities and levels of activity : 

                            
                            
    SELECT 

    avg ( TotalSteps ) as mean_total_steps,
    avg ((VeryActiveMinutes+FairlyActiveMinutes+SedentaryMinutes+LightlyActiveMinutes)/60) as mean_hours_logged_in,
    avg ( SedentaryMinutes/60) as mean_sedentary_hours,
    avg (FairlyActiveMinutes ) as mean_fairly_active_minutes,
    avg (LightlyActiveMinutes /60) as mean_lightly_active_hours,
    avg (VeryActiveMinutes) as mean_very_active_minutes

    FROM `bellabeat-393115.daily_activity.daily_acitvity_updated_csv` as daily_activity
    INNER JOIN `bellabeat-393115.daily_activity.sleep_data` as sleep_data 
    ON daily_activity.Id = sleep_data.Id AND daily_activity.ActivityDate = sleep_data.ActivityDate
    Result:

    This gives us important insights about the dataset.

    • The mean steps taken by 30 candidates in the given timeframe is around 8500 steps per day, which is lesser than the recommended 10000 steps per day.
    • The mean time logged by the tracker in 16.2 hours out of which :
      1. Mean time spent sedentary takes up the most - almost 12 hours per day
      2. Mean time spent lightly active - about 3.61 hours per day
      3. Mean time spent fairly active - about only 18 minutes per day
      4. Mean time spent very active - about 25 minutes per day

      • This is important data as it gives information about how an average user spends their day. Based on this information, certain imporvements can be done to the tracker and the application to improve the performance.

F: Acting based on the insights

From the trends observed because of the data analysis, we got a lot of insights on the fitness tracker industry. These insights can be used to identify potential growth opportunities for Bellabeat.

  • From the Statistical calculations performed on 'daily_activity' database, we got to know that average steps taken per day is around 8500, which is below the recommended 10000 steps per day. Moreover, the mean time spent sedentary takes up most of the day. Therefore, the Bellabeat app can be made such that the users receive periodic notifications encouraging them to stay active.
  • We got to know that the number of log-ins to the fitness app is the least on the week-ends. Also, more calories are burnt when more steps are taken everyday. These challenges also can be solved by notifying the users to stay active in the weekends and to finish their step goals. Introducing points system where new points are granted for every achievement succeded can greatly motivate the users.
  • The points system can be set to reward the users when a given set of achievements are completed per day - like completing step goals, staying hydrated, having quality sleep, etc.
  • Some weekly goals can also be set, like weight loss goals, which can further encourage the users to stay fit and active.
  • Also, Bellabeat app can educate and encourage the users by providing free fitness programs, diet plans and calorie charts.
